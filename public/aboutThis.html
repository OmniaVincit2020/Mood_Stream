Mood Mapper Beta shows you a worldwide map of tweets, represented as colored circles, about any subject you'd like to see. Number of followers sets a circle's size, and the mood of the tweet sets the color: blue shows positivity, and red, negativity. Circles are centered at their tweet's origin.

To learn more about how the tweets are translated into mood, check out this article, written by the creator of the AFINN word list. In short, this app's analysis looks for AFINN words with a positive ("love") or negative ("hate") connotation, then increases red and/or blue accordingly. The brightness of a color will show you how extreme that mood is. You'll see a lot of gray tweets; those are the ones that did not have any AFINN words.

If you'd like to see the words that are used in this app and their scores, click here. It's the AFINN list, plus a few additions. Heads up: NSFW. F-bombs and such.

You'll have your choice of either live streaming or searching tweets up to a week old. Click 'Go to Search' to start, click 'Mapping Tips' to see all the map's features -- and, enjoy!
