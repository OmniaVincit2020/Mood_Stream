When you...
move the cursor over a circle:its info window opens until you move the cursor away.
click once on a circle:its info window stays open.
double-click on a circle:the circle hides.
move the cursor off a circle:its info window hides -- unless you clicked the circle. If you want the info window to go away, move the cursor over the circle again, then move it off. When the cursor leaves, the info window goes back into hiding.
drag the circle:the info window hides, but is following the circle behind the scenes.
release the circle, after a drag:the info window reappears at the circle's new location.
click a tweet in the text crawl: the map pans to that tweet's circle.
move the cursor over a tweet in the crawl: a yellow ring zooms in and out around this tweet's circle, showing you where it is on the map. If you see the gray box south of Hawaii turn yellow, that means the tweet had no location, so was put in the box.
see the entire map covered by one color: double-click anywhere and this mega-tweet will hide.
can't find a tweet on the map: click the crawl text, and if that doesn't work, it may not have reached the map before you paused. Finding the coordinates requires an outside service to return a result, which takes a moment or two.
